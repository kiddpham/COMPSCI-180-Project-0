Technical Analysis

Focal length is defined as the distance between the sensor and a lens' optical center. A change in this property will alter the perspective of the photo. When a photo's perspective is altered, it changes the proportion between subjects in a photo.

In the context of faces, the distance between the lens and the face can significantly affect the proportions of facial features. When taken up close, the nose, ears, and other protruding facial features are more pronounced. This makes the face look less appealing than when captured further away.

In contrast, when the face is further away from the lens and we zoom in, the focal length is effectively lengthened. In turn, features slightly flatten together, making the face look overall more appealing and natural.

Dolly Zoom Analysis

The dolly zoom is a specialized camera technique that combines two opposing movements: physically moving the camera closer to or further from the subject while simultaneously adjusting the zoom to keep the subject the same size in frame.

This technique creates a dramatic visual effect where the subject remains constant but the background appears to compress or expand, creating an unsettling perspective shift that's been used in films like "Vertigo" and "Jaws" to convey psychological tension.

The animation demonstrates how the background architecture changes dramatically while the foreground subject maintains consistent framing, showcasing the relationship between focal length and perspective compression in a dynamic way.
